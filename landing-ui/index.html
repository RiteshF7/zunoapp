Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Zuno.

How does "Share to Zuno" work? expand_more
When you see content in Instagram, YouTube, Twitter, or any app, tap the native Share button and select Zuno from the share sheet. The content is instantly saved to your Zuno library with all metadata (title, source, thumbnail). Works on both Android and iOS.
What does AI categorization mean? expand_more
When content is saved, AI analyzes it and automatically assigns tags (e.g., #recipe, #tech, #travel), a brief summary, and places it in the appropriate collection. You can also add your own tags. No manual filing needed.
What is the personalized feed? expand_more
The feed shows you new, related content from across social media — not your saved items, but new discoveries. It's curated based on your personality inferred from what you save. Videos, articles, podcasts, posts, all in one scrollable feed.
Is my data private? expand_more
Yes. Zuno is built with privacy first. Your saved content and preferences are encrypted. We don't sell your data. AI processing happens in a secure environment. You own your data and can export or delete anytime.
Which platforms are supported? expand_more
Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, TikTok, Reddit, Pinterest, Spotify, Medium, and any app with a Share action. We also plan a browser extension for saving articles and web content.
Is Zuno free? expand_more
Zuno will offer a free tier with generous limits. A Pro plan will unlock unlimited storage, advanced AI features, collaborative collections, and priority support. Join the waitlist to get early access and special pricing.
